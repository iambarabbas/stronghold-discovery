Content Strategy Recommendations

Priority 1: Create Intent-Mapped Content (Weeks 4-8)

Build 10-15 pages targeting non-branded search queries aligned with donor intent:

Crisis Response Intent (reactive, time-sensitive)

  • "Burma Conflict Relief — How Emergency Evacuations Work"
  • "Veteran-Led Rescue Operations in Active War Zones"
  • "Afghanistan Evacuation: What Happened and What's Next"

Effectiveness Seeker Intent (research-driven)

  • "How Humanitarian Extractions Are Conducted"
  • "Why Veteran-Led Teams Succeed in Conflict Zones"
  • "Is Stronghold Rescue & Relief Legitimate?" (trust page)
  • "Charity With Dignity: A Different Approach to Aid"

Protect the Innocent Intent (emotional entry)

  • "Refugee Protection in Burma: What's Really Happening"
  • "Persecution in Myanmar: How Rescue Teams Respond"
  • "Families in Conflict Zones: Stories of Rescue"

Priority 2: Earn Contextual Editorial Links (Weeks 5-12)

Target 10-20 contextual, topically-relevant backlinks with descriptive anchors:

  • Journalist Outreach: Pitch mission updates as primary sources to journalists covering Burma/Afghanistan
  • Military/Veteran Publications: Task & Purpose, Coffee or Die, The War Horse
  • Religious/Humanitarian Directories: Editorial links from aligned organization directories
  • Unlinked Mention Conversion: HARO responses, podcast mentions that don't currently link

Priority 3: Build Internal Content Hubs

Create topical authority clusters that pass internal link equity strategically:

Hub 1: Rescue Operations

Mission stories + operation updates → link upward to "How We Operate" explainer → link to monthly giving CTA

Hub 2: Regional Impact

Burma/Afghanistan/Venezuela country pages → link to specific rescue stories → link to relevant donation campaign

Hub 3: Trust & Transparency

FAQ, financial transparency, impact metrics → link to "Why Us" page → link to donation page
